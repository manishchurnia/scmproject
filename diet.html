Pasta with Pesto & Tomatoes

This quick and delicious pasta dish is the perfect way to use up a summer bounty of basil and tomatoes!

Don't have a green thumb? Never fear! You can knock out this classic pasta dish in no time with a jar of prepared pesto sauce.

  • Servings4
  • Prep Time5 min
  • Cook Time25 min
  • Total Time30 min

The Ingredients

Variation: Got no time, or no fresh basil? Use a 6 ounce jar of prepared pesto instead.

The Process

  1. Bring 4 quarts of water to a rolling boil in a large pot. Salt the water generously and add the pasta.
  2. While the pasta cooks, make the pesto.
    • Remove stems from basil leaves.
    • Put the basil, garlic, and pine nuts or almonds into the bowl of a food processor and pulse it a few times to chop them up.
    • Then, turn the food processor on and slowly pour the olive oil through the feeder tube to blend with the chopped herbs and nuts.
    • Process until all ingredients are fully blended, stopping the food processor to scrape down the sides occasionally to get it all mixed.
    • Turn off the food processor, and add the salt and Parmesean, then pulse a few times to blend.
  3. Heat a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add the 1/2 tsp. olive oil to the heated skillet.
  5. Add the cherry tomatoes to the skillet and let them blister, stirring occasionally and gently so they don't burst.
  6. Remove tomatoes from the pan and set aside.
  7. Drain the pasta, reserving 1/4 cup of the cooking water.
  8. Return the pasta to the pot
  9. Add the reserved cooking water and pesto to the pasta and stir to mix
  10. Plate the pasta and add 6-8 tomatoes to each plate
  11. Garnish with reserved basil leaves and Parmesean and serve. Buon Appetito!

Overnight Chia Pudding

Overnight chia pudding is a healthy, no-cook recipe where chia seeds absorb milk to create a creamy, nutrient-rich dish. It's perfect for a quick, customizable breakfast or snack topped with fruits, nuts, or granola.

  • Servings2
  • Prep Time5 min
  • Cook TimeNone
  • Total Time6 hours 5 minutes (including refrigeration time)

The Ingredients

The Process

  1. Mix the Base:In a jar or bowl, combine the chia seeds and milk. Add vanilla extract and sweetener if desired.
  2. Stir Thoroughly:Whisk or stir the mixture well to ensure the chia seeds are evenly distributed and don’t clump together.
  3. Rest and Stir Again:After 5-10 minutes, stir the mixture again to break up any clumps that may have formed.
  4. Refrigerate Overnight:Cover the jar or bowl and refrigerate for at least 6 hours or overnight. During this time, the chia seeds will absorb the liquid and form a pudding-like consistency
  5. Add Toppings and Serve:In the morning, give the pudding a quick stir and top it with fresh fruits (e.g., berries, banana slices), nuts, granola, or any toppings you like.

Spinach & Lentil Soup

A hearty, protein-rich soup that’s packed with fiber and flavor. This comforting dish is easy to prepare, perfect for meal prep, and incredibly nourishing

  • Servings4
  • Prep Time10 min
  • Cook Time30 min
  • Total Time40 min

The Ingredients

The Process

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Stir in garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  2. Cook the Lentils: Add the lentils to the pot, stirring to coat them in the oil and aromatics. Pour in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes. Add cumin, turmeric, smoked paprika, and chili flakes if using. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender
  3. Add the Spinach: Stir in the spinach and let it wilt for about 2-3 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and serve hot with a wedge of lemon for a burst of freshness. Pair with crusty bread or a side salad for a complete meal.

Zucchini Noodles with Tomato Basil Sauce

This light and flavorful dish features zucchini noodles (also known as "zoodles") paired with a vibrant and fresh tomato basil sauce. It’s a perfect low-carb, gluten-free alternative to pasta that’s full of nutrients and quick to prepare.

  • Servings2
  • Prep Time10 min
  • Cook Time10 min
  • Total Time20 min

The Ingredients

The Process

  1. Prepare the Zucchini Noodles:
    • Use a spiralizer to turn the zucchinis into noodles. If you don’t have a spiralizer, you can use a julienne peeler or simply slice the zucchinis into thin strips with a knife.
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the zucchini noodles and sauté them for 2-3 minutes, just until they’re tender but still have some bite. Be careful not to overcook, as they can become soggy.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. Make the Tomato Basil Sauce:
    • In the same skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Add the halved cherry tomatoes (or canned diced tomatoes) to the skillet. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes soften and release their juices.
    • Stir in the balsamic vinegar (optional) and chopped fresh basil. Continue to cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Assemble the Dish:
    • Add the sautéed zucchini noodles to the skillet with the tomato basil sauce. Toss everything together until the zucchini noodles are well coated with the sauce.
    • Serve immediately, garnished with extra basil if desired. You can also sprinkle some Parmesan cheese or nutritional yeast on top for added flavor.

Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

These stuffed mushrooms make a delicious, healthy dish that’s perfect for a light lunch, dinner, or a satisfying appetizer. Let me know if you'd like more variations!

  • Servings4
  • Prep Time10 min
  • Cook Time20-25 min
  • Total Time30-35 min

The Ingredients

The Process

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the Mushrooms: Clean the portobello mushrooms with a damp paper towel. Carefully remove the stems and scrape out the gills using a spoon to create space for the stuffing.
  3. Sauté the Spinach: Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Make the Filling: In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, sautéed spinach, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well until everything is fully combined.
  5. Stuff the Mushrooms: Spoon the spinach and ricotta mixture into the mushroom caps, pressing gently to pack the filling in.
  6. Bake: Place the stuffed mushrooms on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden and slightly bubbly.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired. Serve warm as an appetizer or a main dish with a side salad or roasted vegetables.
